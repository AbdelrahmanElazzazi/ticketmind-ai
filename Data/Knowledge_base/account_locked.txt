Account Locked Procedure

User accounts may become temporarily locked when multiple unsuccessful login attempts are detected. The lockout mechanism protects accounts against unauthorized access attempts and credential-based attacks.

Accounts are automatically locked after five consecutive failed login attempts within a 15-minute period. Once locked, access is blocked for 15 minutes.

Users should avoid repeated login attempts during the lockout period because additional attempts may extend the lock duration.

Support agents may manually unlock accounts after verifying user identity using approved verification methods. Verification typically includes confirming user information, registered email address, and recent account activity.

Common Causes:

- Forgotten password
- Expired credentials
- Incorrect username
- Browser autofill errors
- Unauthorized login attempts

Troubleshooting Process:

1. Verify username.
2. Confirm password reset is not required.
3. Wait for automatic unlock period.
4. Perform manual unlock if necessary.
5. Review security logs for suspicious activity.

Security Considerations:

If suspicious login activity is detected from unfamiliar locations or devices, the account should remain locked until additional verification is completed.

Support agents must document all manual unlock actions within the ticketing system.

Expected Resolution Time:
15 minutes for automatic unlock or less than 30 minutes for manual review.
